Few people associate Algeria with volcanoes, yet the deep Sahara holds dramatic
volcanic fields — eroded plugs, cones, and lava domes rising from desert
plateaus. Most cluster in the Hoggar (Ahaggar) massif around Tamanrasset.

## 1. Atakor volcanic field

A high plateau studded with phonolite plugs and necks — the eroded throats of
ancient volcanoes — that form the iconic Hoggar skyline.

## 2. Assekrem

The most famous viewpoint in the Atakor, a volcanic high point whose sunrise
over the plug landscape is legendary among desert travellers.

## 3. Ilamane

A sheer volcanic spire near Assekrem, one of the most photographed natural
landmarks in the Sahara.

## 4. Tahat

The highest peak in Algeria, rising from the volcanic Hoggar massif — the roof
of the Sahara.

## 5. Adrar Hadriane

A prominent volcanic summit in the Atakor group, a landmark for routes across
the plateau.

## 6. Tin Tarabine area cones

Eroded volcanic relief on the eastern Hoggar, remote and rarely visited.

## 7. Manzaz volcanic field

A younger volcanic field northeast of the Hoggar with well-preserved scoria
cones and lava flows.

## 8. In Ezzane / eastern Hoggar plugs

Isolated volcanic necks marking the desert's eastern reaches.

## 9. Tahalra volcanic field

A southern Hoggar field of cones and flows on the route toward the Tassili.

## 10. Hoggar phonolite domes (general)

Across the massif, dozens of unnamed domes and plugs make the entire region one
extended volcanic monument.

## Why Algeria's volcanoes are special

These are not erupting volcanoes — they are deeply eroded ones. Millions of
years of desert weathering stripped the soft slopes away and left the hard
volcanic cores standing as towers and spires. The result is a landscape that
looks sculpted rather than built.

## Visiting the Hoggar

Tamanrasset is the gateway. Travel into the Atakor requires a licensed local
agency, permits, a 4x4, and a guide — this is deep desert with no
infrastructure. Trips are typically organised expeditions of several days,
camping under exceptional dark skies. Independent travel is not advisable or
generally permitted.

## Timing and safety

Go October to March; summer Saharan heat is dangerous. Carry far more water
than you think, follow your guide on routes and security advice, and respect
Tuareg communities and sites. Take nothing — these volcanic landscapes are
protected within Ahaggar National Park.
