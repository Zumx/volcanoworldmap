The Galápagos archipelago, on the equator about 1,000 kilometres
west of Ecuador, is one of the world's most productive ocean-island
hot spots. Most of its big islands are single shield volcanoes;
several erupt every decade or so, producing the wide,
gently-sloping summits that Darwin saw and then revisited in
"Origin of Species."

## 1. Sierra Negra

A vast shield volcano on Isabela Island, with one of the largest
calderas in the world by area. The 2018 eruption produced fluid
basaltic flows that ran for weeks. The rim hike from Puerto Villamil
is one of the best volcanic experiences in the archipelago.

## 2. Wolf

Also on Isabela, the highest peak in the Galápagos at 1,710 m.
Eruptions in 2015 and 2022 sent flows down the southern flank
into territory inhabited only by pink iguanas — the species lives
exclusively here.

## 3. Cerro Azul

The southernmost volcano of Isabela. A 2008 eruption produced
spectacular lava fountains in the caldera. Cerro Azul tortoises
on its slopes were rescued during eruptions in earlier decades.

## 4. Alcedo

Central Isabela's shield volcano, with a striking 7-km caldera
holding hot springs and large herds of giant tortoises. Trails
into the caldera are sometimes open for limited multi-day
hikes.

## 5. Darwin

Northern Isabela, named for the obvious reason. A long quiet
period has been broken by occasional eruptions, including a
significant event in 1813. The caldera is filled with a saltwater
lake.

## 6. Ecuador

The smallest of Isabela's five shields, on the island's
northwest. A 1981 eruption produced ash and flows. Ecuador
volcano is also a frequent point of reference in Galápagos
biogeography studies.

## 7. Fernandina (La Cumbre)

A single, almost circular shield occupying the whole island of
Fernandina. One of the world's most active basaltic volcanoes,
erupting in 2009, 2017, 2018, 2020 and 2024. The caldera holds a
lake that drained spectacularly in 1968.

## 8. Santiago

A shield with a long-history rift zone in the centre of the
archipelago. The 1759 eruption is one of the earliest written
records by visiting sailors. The island has Sullivan Bay's
striking 1897 pāhoehoe lava field.

## 9. Pinta

The northernmost main island. Famous as the home of Lonesome
George, last of the Pinta tortoises, the volcano itself has
been quiet for centuries; the rim is dry and shrubby.

## 10. Marchena

A small island in the north of the archipelago, with a 1991
eruption that gave the first satellite-monitored basaltic
event in the chain. Otherwise unvisited; the cone is offshore-only.

## How to plan a Galápagos volcano trip

Most visits are by cruise. Few cruises actively visit volcanoes —
the wildlife is the standard draw — but Sierra Negra is a
common day hike from Puerto Villamil, and overflights of active
Fernandina are sometimes possible.
