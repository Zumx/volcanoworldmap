In the middle of vast Lake Nicaragua, the largest lake in Central America, rises one
of the most striking volcanic landscapes in the world: Ometepe Island, formed by two
volcanoes joined by a low isthmus. The active, near-perfect cone of Concepcion and
the dormant, forest-cloaked Maderas together create an hourglass-shaped island whose
fertile soils, ancient petroglyphs, and dramatic scenery have made it a place of
myth, agriculture, and growing tourism.

## An island built of two volcanoes

Ometepe Island was created entirely by volcanism, formed where two volcanoes rose
from the floor of Lake Nicaragua and their bases eventually merged into a single
landmass. The result is a distinctive figure-eight or hourglass shape, with the two
cones connected by a narrow neck of land. It is the largest volcanic island in a
freshwater lake in the world, an extraordinary geological setting.

## Concepcion, the active cone

Concepcion is the higher and active of Ometepe's two volcanoes, a near-perfectly
symmetrical stratovolcano reaching about 1,610 metres. It has erupted numerous times
in recent history, with explosions and ash emissions, and remains one of
Nicaragua's active volcanoes. Its graceful cone dominates the northern half of the
island, often crowned with cloud or, during activity, a plume of ash.

## Maderas, the dormant twin

Maderas, the southern volcano, is lower and considered dormant, its slopes cloaked
in lush cloud forest. Reaching about 1,394 metres, it holds a crater lake at its
summit, a tranquil counterpoint to the active Concepcion. The contrast between the
two volcanoes, one barren and active, the other forested and quiet, gives Ometepe
its unique character.

## Fertile volcanic soils

The volcanic soils of Ometepe are exceptionally fertile, supporting agriculture
that has sustained communities on the island for millennia. Plantains, coffee,
cacao, and other crops thrive in the rich volcanic earth. This fertility, born of
the volcanoes' ash and weathered rock, is the foundation of life on the island and
a vivid example of how volcanism enriches the land.

## A landscape of myth and history

Ometepe has been inhabited for thousands of years, and its volcanoes feature in
Indigenous cosmology and legend. The island is rich in pre-Columbian petroglyphs
and stone sculptures, carved by peoples who lived in the shadow of the volcanoes
long before European contact. This deep human history, intertwined with the
volcanic landscape, adds a profound cultural dimension to the island.

## A growing destination

In recent decades, Ometepe has become an increasingly popular destination for
travellers drawn by its volcanoes, forests, beaches, and tranquil atmosphere.
Hikers climb both Concepcion and Maderas, while others explore the island's
wildlife, archaeological sites, and natural springs. This tourism has become an
important part of the local economy, built around the island's volcanic identity.

## Living between two volcanoes

For the people of Ometepe, life unfolds between an active and a dormant volcano.
Concepcion's eruptions are a periodic reminder of the island's living geology, while
Maderas offers its forests and crater lake. The community balances the benefits of
fertile soil and tourism with awareness of the active volcano at the island's
heart, monitored as part of Nicaragua's volcano network.
