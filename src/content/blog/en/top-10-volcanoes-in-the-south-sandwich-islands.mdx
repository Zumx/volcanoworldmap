Few volcanic regions on Earth are as wild, as remote and as poorly documented as the South Sandwich Islands. This crescent of eleven small islands curves southward from 57°S to 59°S in the South Atlantic, roughly 2,000 km east of the Falkland Islands and about 470 km southeast of South Georgia. The islands are uninhabited, visited only by occasional scientific expeditions and tour ships, and administered as a British Overseas Territory. Yet they constitute one of the Southern Ocean's most volcanically active arcs, with eruptions that can only be confirmed by satellite — no one lives close enough to witness them firsthand.

## Mount Michael, Saunders Island

Mount Michael is the most active of the South Sandwich Island volcanoes and one of the most remarkable volcano discoveries of the satellite era. In 2019, analysis of Sentinel-2 and Landsat imagery confirmed the presence of a persistent lava lake in its summit crater, making it one of approximately ten known active lava lakes on Earth. Temperatures measured by satellite-mounted thermal instruments reach over 1,000°C at the lake surface. The island rises steeply from the sea to 990 m; no land access is possible without specialist polar expedition equipment.

## Zavodovski Island

Zavodovski is a low volcanic island at the northern end of the chain, dominated by Mount Curry (554 m). It is best known not for its volcanism but for its penguin colony — one of the largest in the world, with an estimated two million chinstrap penguins nesting on its slopes. Fumarolic activity is continuous on Mount Curry's flanks, and steam plumes are visible from passing ships. An eruption was confirmed by satellite imagery in 2016.

## Montagu Island

Montagu is the largest of the South Sandwich Islands and rises to 1,370 m at its highest point, making it the tallest peak in the chain. Its summit caldera has shown thermal anomalies detected by infrared satellite sensors, suggesting an active or recently active magma body beneath. Lava flows have reached the coast on the northern and southern sides of the island within the past century.

## Candlemas Island

Candlemas Island is a twin-peaked volcanic island at the northern end of the group, reaching 576 m. Its two summits — East Peak and West Peak — are separated by a saddle. Fumaroles are active on the upper slopes. The surrounding sea is often rough, preventing close approach even by ice-hardened research vessels.

## Visokoi Island

Visokoi Island sits in the middle of the chain with its main peak reaching 755 m. Satellite data has revealed fresh lava flows on its flanks, and its summit shows persistent high temperatures. The island is known to have experienced at least one eruption in the 20th century, based on bathymetric anomalies on the seafloor around it.

## Leskov Island

Leskov is the smallest island in the South Sandwich group, roughly 1 km across and rising to 190 m. Despite its tiny footprint, thermal sensors have detected elevated heat flux from its surface, indicating ongoing volcanic activity beneath. It is often ice-covered in winter.

## Traversay Islands

The Traversay Islands comprise three small volcanic islands — Bellingshausen, Syzygy and Pobeda — at the southern end of the chain. All show geomorphological evidence of recent volcanism: fresh lava benches, fumarolic venting at sea level and hydrothermal discolouration of the surrounding water. No confirmed eruptions are documented in the historical record but eruptions there may simply go undetected.

## Bristol Island

Bristol Island (1,100 m) is one of the more imposing peaks in the chain. A 1935 eruption was reported by a passing ship, one of the few historical observations of an eruption in the South Sandwich Islands made before the satellite era. The island is covered by glaciers that cascade to the coast, and the interaction of volcanic heat with ice creates persistent steam clouds above the summit.

## Thule Island Group

At the southern end of the chain, the Thule Island Group consists of three peaks — Southern Thule, Cook Island and Bellingshausen — on a single submarine plateau. The group was the site of a brief and somewhat controversial Argentine military presence in the late 1970s. Fumaroles are active on Southern Thule, and thermal anomalies have been detected periodically by satellites.

## Submarine Volcanoes of the Southern Arc

Beneath the waters surrounding the South Sandwich Islands, numerous submarine volcanic peaks and ridges complete the arc. Bathymetric surveys have identified at least six significant submarine volcanic edifices between and south of the emerged islands. These include caldera structures and fresh lava fields on the seafloor, suggesting ongoing submarine eruptions that may occasionally create temporary shallow-water shoals.
