Costa Rica is small but volcanically rich. The Cordillera de Guanacaste,
Cordillera Central and Cordillera de Tilarán carry a chain of active and
dormant volcanoes through the heart of the country, several within an
hour or two of San José.

## 1. Arenal

The classic Costa Rican volcano — a perfect cone above La Fortuna that
erupted continuously from 1968 to 2010 and is now quiet but warm. Hot
springs at its base remain the country's most popular evening activity.

## 2. Poás

A drive-up active volcano less than an hour from San José, with a vast
acidic crater lake. Access is closed during eruption phases; on calm
days a short walk leads to the rim viewpoint.

## 3. Irazú

Costa Rica's highest volcano (3,432 m), with green and silver crater
lakes and a road to the rim. A drive-up view on clear days reaches both
the Atlantic and the Pacific.

## 4. Turrialba

The active volcano east of San José, with regular ash columns. Closed
during eruption phases and reachable only by 4×4 in calm periods through
its national park.

## 5. Rincón de la Vieja

The northern Guanacaste volcano, a long ridge of craters, fumaroles, mud
pots and waterfalls. The national park has the country's best volcanic
hiking.

## 6. Miravalles

A geothermal volcano west of Rincón with mud baths, hot springs and
several geothermal power plants on its slopes — accessible from Bagaces.

## 7. Tenorio

Famous for the surreal turquoise Río Celeste, a river coloured by
volcanic minerals on Tenorio's flank. The waterfall walk is one of the
country's icons.

## 8. Barva

The dormant volcano above Heredia, climbed through Braulio Carrillo
National Park for cloud-forest hikes to a peaceful crater lake.

## 9. Orosí

The northernmost of the Costa Rican volcanoes, near the Nicaraguan
border in Guanacaste — a dormant pair (Orosí–Cacao) inside Guanacaste
National Park.

## 10. Platanar / Porvenir cluster

Less-known volcanoes north of Poás, dormant but climbable as quiet
alternatives to the more crowded peaks.

## How to plan a Costa Rica volcano trip

A week is enough for the highlights: Arenal and its hot springs, a day
at Poás, Río Celeste at Tenorio, and a long day in Rincón de la Vieja.
San José makes a logical base for the central volcanoes.

## Hazard and access

OVSICORI monitors the active volcanoes and issues bulletins. Poás and
Turrialba are the most often closed; check before driving up. National
parks publish opening times on their own pages, which can change with
short notice.

## See them on the map

as a north-west to south-east line through the country. Most volcanoes
