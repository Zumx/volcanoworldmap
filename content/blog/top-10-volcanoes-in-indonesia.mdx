Indonesia has more active volcanoes than any country on the planet — over a
hundred — strung along the Ring of Fire. These ten are the most extraordinary.

## 1. Mount Bromo, Java

A smoking cone in a vast "Sea of Sand" caldera — the most famous sunrise in
Indonesia.

## 2. Ijen, Java

Famous for its electric-blue burning sulphur flames and a turquoise acid
crater lake worked by sulphur miners.

## 3. Krakatoa (Anak Krakatau), Sunda Strait

The child of the 1883 eruption — one of the most violent in recorded history —
still actively growing.

## 4. Mount Rinjani, Lombok

A towering caldera volcano with a crater lake and a cone within it — a classic
multi-day trek.

## 5. Mount Merapi, Java

One of the most active and dangerous volcanoes in the world, looming over
Yogyakarta.

## 6. Mount Agung, Bali

Bali's sacred high volcano, central to the island's spiritual life.

## 7. Mount Batur, Bali

An accessible caldera volcano famous for an easy sunrise hike.

## 8. Tambora, Sumbawa

Site of the largest eruption in recorded history (1815), which caused a global
"year without a summer."

## 9. Kelimutu, Flores

Three crater lakes that change colour independently — surreal and sacred.

## 10. Kerinci, Sumatra

The highest volcano in Indonesia, towering over rainforest and tea estates.

## How to visit Indonesian volcanoes

Most popular volcanoes (Bromo, Ijen, Batur, Kelimutu) are guided sunrise hikes
with local operators; bigger ones (Rinjani, Kerinci) are multi-day treks
requiring guides and permits. Conditions and access change with volcanic
activity — Indonesia's volcano monitoring agency (PVMBG) sets alert levels that
close mountains without notice.

## Timing and hazards

The dry season (roughly April–October) is best for access and clear sunrises.
The real hazards are volcanic gas (Ijen's crater can be lethal — heed guides
and gas masks), sudden activity, and steep loose terrain. Never enter a closed
or restricted volcano.

## Respect culture and safety

Many volcanoes are sacred (Agung, Bromo, Kelimutu) — follow local customs and
ceremonies, stay on routes, use licensed guides, and obey alert levels
absolutely. Take nothing and tread lightly.
